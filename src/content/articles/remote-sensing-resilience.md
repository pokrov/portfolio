excerpt: "Satellite imagery can make urban growth, environmental pressure, and climate exposure more visible to planners and engineers."
---

Remote sensing gives urban teams a way to observe change consistently across large territories. For fast-growing cities, this matters because many planning questions involve patterns that are difficult to understand from administrative records alone.

Satellite imagery can help identify expansion fronts, vegetation loss, heat exposure, informal growth, flood-prone areas, and infrastructure pressure. Combined with field knowledge, these signals can support more resilient planning choices.

The value is strongest when analysis is translated into operational language: risk maps, project priorities, monitoring indicators, and evidence that can be reused by local institutions.

For Morocco, the challenge is to connect technical capacity with governance. Data products should not remain isolated reports; they should become part of how territories diagnose problems, compare scenarios, and monitor public action over time.
